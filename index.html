About Me

I graduated with a Computer Science degree from UTRGV in 2020 and currently work as a QA Software Analyst in Harlingen, TX. In my free time, I dive into side projects because I’m obsessed with building software people enjoy using. My biggest strength is my relentless attention to detail—whether it’s fine-tuning a user interface or optimizing a workflow, I focus on delivering the best possible experience for users. Technically, I’m most comfortable with Angular, .NET backends, and MySQL, but I never hesitate to automate any tedious task with whatever tool the sitation calls for. I’m also a big believer in thoughtful design and solid architecture; I enjoy reasoning through solutions to find the most optimal path forward. Above all, I’m not afraid to speak up about the small improvements that can make a product sing. If something’s mediocre, I’ll challenge it—respectfully, but firmly—because I truly believe great software emerges only when we refuse to settle

I believe in coding as simply as possible—without sacrificing performance or user experience. t’s about building solutions that feel effortless for the people who use them. If that means writing more lines of code in a straightforward way, so be it. I code for users because, in the end, user experience is king! Outside of coding, I’m big on music—so big that I built RarelyGroovy.com to catalog local artists and make discovering new bands a delight. I love diving into album art, reading about how bands formed, and connecting the dots between different projects. Ultimately, whether I’m exploring music or writing code, I’m driven by the same goal: crafting experiences people genuinely enjoy.
